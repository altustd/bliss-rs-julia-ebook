BLISS-2026

Decomposition-Based Multidisciplinary Design Optimization — A Modern Julia Treatment

Author

Troy David Altus

Published

May 1, 2026

About This Book

BLISS-2026 is an updated thesis that takes the Bi-Level Integrated System Synthesis (BLISS) framework from its 2002 origins into the modern era of surrogate-based optimization, Gaussian processes, and high-performance Julia computing.

The starting point is the author’s 2002 Master’s thesis completed at George Washington University under Dr. Jaroslaw Sobieszczanski-Sobieski at NASA Langley Research Center, published as NASA Contractor Report NASA/CR-2002-211652 [1]. That work introduced BLISS-RS: quadratic response surface surrogates as the coupling mechanism between local disciplinary optimizations and a global system optimizer.

Twenty-four years later, the surrogate-modeling landscape has changed fundamentally. This book asks: if you were designing BLISS today, what would you do differently?

0.1 What Changed Since 2002

The 2002 implementation used quadratic polynomials as surrogates because they were cheap to fit, analytically differentiable, and computationally trivial. Those properties still matter — but the toolkit has expanded significantly:

Then (2002) Now (2026)
Quadratic polynomial surrogates Gaussian Process (Kriging) surrogates
Least-squares fit, fixed once Marginal-likelihood hyperparameter tuning
D-Optimal DOE, fixed budget Latin Hypercube + adaptive Expected Improvement
Nelder-Mead over polynomial Bayesian optimization with EI acquisition
No uncertainty estimates Full predictive distributions
Serial BB evaluation Parallel Threads.@threads in Julia
Fixed 20% interval shrinkage Trust-region bounds management

0.2 What This Book Contains

The first eight chapters closely follow the original 2002 thesis — faithfully reproduced with all algorithms implemented in Julia. They establish the classical BLISS-RS framework in complete, executable detail.

The modern contribution begins at Chapter 9:

  • Chapter 9 — BLISS-2026 Framework: The full Gaussian-process-based redesign. GP theory, kernel functions, marginal likelihood, Expected Improvement acquisition, and a complete working implementation that runs the supersonic business jet benchmark. Direct comparison against BLISS-RS shows where GP surrogates win and where they add cost.

  • Chapter 10 — SRM Application: BLISS-RS and BLISS-2026 both applied to solid propellant rocket motor conceptual design — connecting to the author’s propulsion engineering background at ATK Elkton, MD.

  • Chapter 11 — Modern MDO Landscape: A survey of where MDO decomposition is heading: multi-fidelity co-kriging, neural-network surrogates, uncertainty quantification, and digital-twin integration.
